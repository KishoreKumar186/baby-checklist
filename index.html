0/0
 
 
   
     
✓
     
       
Baby bath tub
       
LuvLapMee Mee
       
Get one with a newborn sling/insert so you have both hands free. Anti-slip base is important.
     
     
Must
   
 
 
   
     
✓
     
       
Mild baby wash + shampoo (2-in-1)
       
The Moms Co NaturalMamaearth GentleCetaphil Baby
       
No sulfates, no parabens, tear-free formula. Cetaphil is dermatologist-recommended and most gentle for eczema-prone skin.
        💡 What to look for ▾        
Look for: Tear-free, pH-balanced (around 5.5), no artificial fragrance, no SLS/SLES. For the first month, plain water is actually sufficient for most days — save bath wash for 2–3x a week.
     
     
Must
   
 
 
   
     
✓
     
       
Baby moisturizer / lotion
       
The Moms CoCetaphil BabyHimalaya Gentle
       
Newborn skin peels naturally in week 1–2. A gentle daily moisturizer helps. Avoid coconut oil for first 4 weeks — it can disrupt skin barrier for some newborns.
     
     
Must
   
 
 
   
     
✓
     
       
Diaper rash cream
       
Sebamed BabyChicco Bum CreamHimalaya Diaper Rash
       
Apply at every change to prevent rash before it starts. Look for zinc oxide as active ingredient.
     
     
Must
   
 
 
   
     
✓
     
       
Soft hooded towels (2)
       
Moms Co BambooSnugkins

🌸 Post-Delivery Recovery
   
0/0
 
 
   
     
✓
     
       
Maternity pads (2–3 packs)
       
Whisper Overnight XLStayfree Secure Nights
       
Lochia (post-delivery bleeding) can last 4–6 weeks. Stock up. Change every 3–4 hours. Avoid tampons completely.
     
     
Must
   
 
 
   
     
✓
     
       
Postpartum belly binder / abdominal wrap
       
Morph Maternity BinderPregabelly
       
Helps with back support and abdominal recovery post-vaginal or C-section delivery. Not for shaping — purely for support and pain relief. Wear for first 6 weeks.
        💡 What to look for ▾        
For C-section: Get one with a low-cut design that doesn't press on the incision. Breathable fabric is important — India's heat makes non-breathable binders very uncomfortable.
     
     
Must
   
 
 
   
     
✓
     
       
Sitz bath tub (for vaginal delivery)
       
Generic on Amazon India (~₹250–400)
       
Soaking perineum in warm water 2–3x daily speeds healing of stitches. Fits over toilet seat. Skip if C-section.
     
     
Good
   
 
 
   
     
✓
     
       
Stool softener / fiber supplement
       
Cremaffin Plus (doctor-approved)Naturolax
       
First bowel movement post-delivery is scary for most moms — especially with stitches. Ask your OB-GYN to prescribe a stool softener. Keep high-fiber foods ready.
     
     
Must

Postnatal vitamins (iron + calcium + D3)
       
Shelcal (calcium)Feronia XT (iron)OB Complete

🏡 Before Baby Arrives
   
0/0
 
 
   
     
✓
     
       
Wash all baby clothes before first use
       
Wash with mild baby detergent (not adult detergent) before baby wears them. New fabric has chemical residue.
       
Genteel Baby DetergentPigeon Laundry Detergent
     
     
Must
   
 
 
   
     
✓
     
       
Mosquito protection for sleeping area
       
GoodKnight fabric roll-on (baby-safe)Himalaya Mosquito repellent patches
       
No mats/coils/sprays near baby. Fabric roll-on or patches on clothing are safest for under 3 months.
     
     
Must
   
 
 
   
     
✓
     
       
Thermometer (digital)
       
Omron MC-246Dr. Morepen MT-111
       
Any rectal/axillary digital thermometer works. Fever in a newborn (>100.4°F) is a medical emergency — you need to check quickly and accurately.
     
     
Must
   
 
 
   
     
✓
     
       
Nasal aspirator
       
Pigeon Nasal AspiratorNoseFrida (if budget allows)
       
Babies can't blow their nose. When congested, they can't feed. A nasal aspirator is a lifesaver. NoseFrida is messy but more effective. Pigeon bulb is simpler.
     
     
Must
   
 
 
   
     
✓
     
       
Infant nail scissors / file
       
Chicco safety nail scissorsPigeon nail care set
       
Newborn nails are sharp and grow fast. Baby will scratch their own face. Trim weekly — best done when baby is asleep.
     
     
Must
   
 
 
   
     
✓
     
       
Baby laundry done, area prepped
       
Designate a spot for: diaper station, feeding corner, sleeping area. Having a predictable "station" makes night routines much easier for both of you.
     
     
Must
   
 
 
   
     
✓
     
       
Pediatrician identified & number saved
       
Have your pediatrician's clinic number and an after-hours emergency contact saved before discharge. Baby's first check-up is usually within 3–5 days of birth.
     
     
Must
